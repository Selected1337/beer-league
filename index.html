Projected Top 10 Draft Picks for the Minecraft Hockey Beer League (Based off junior tournament games)

The Minecraft Hockey League (MHL) is gearing up for another exciting season, and with the draft right around the corner, teams are looking to fill their rosters with the best talent available. Based on performance data from previous seasons, here are the projected top 10 draft picks for this year’s draft.

1. **Tendy** - The Offensive Powerhouse

Points (PTS): 144 | Games Played (GP): 43 | Goals (G): 109 | PPG: 3.27

Tendy is the clear frontrunner for the first overall pick. With an incredible 144 points over 43 games, including 109 goals, he is the league's most dominant offensive player. His combination of scoring, playmaking, and physicality (975 hits) makes him a complete player that any team would want on their roster.

2. **Clifford** - The Consistent Contributor

Points (PTS): 88 | Games Played (GP): 28 | Goals (G): 77 | PPG: 3.14

Clifford has earned his spot as the second pick with a balanced and dependable game. His 30 points in just 4 preseason games showcase his ability to dominate in any situation. His physicality (611 hits) and knack for winning faceoffs make him an ideal two-way player.

3. **McChicken** - The Playmaking Sniper

Points (PTS): 60 | Games Played (GP): 28 | Goals (G): 33 | PPG: 2.14

McChicken brings an intelligent playmaking style, feeding his teammates perfect passes while also posing a scoring threat. His 33 goals and 27 assists demonstrate his versatility. Combined with his physicality (780 hits), McChicken is a solid pick for teams seeking an all-around forward.

4. **Birb** - The High-Energy Scorer

Points (PTS): 58 | Games Played (GP): 11 | Goals (G): 35 | PPG: 5.27

Birb is a high-risk, high-reward player, with a jaw-dropping 5.27 PPG over 11 games. His 35 goals and 23 assists show his capability as a game-changing forward. Though his physicality (259 hits) isn't his strongest suit, his offensive numbers make him a top pick for any team in need of scoring depth.

5. **Sammythewhale** - The Two-Way Threat

Points (PTS): 57 | Games Played (GP): 12 | Goals (G): 39 | PPG: 4.75

Sammythewhale is a well-rounded forward who can score and contribute physically. With 39 goals and 18 assists, he has a high PPG of 4.75. His 117 hits show he can play with a gritty edge, making him a great asset for a team that needs both offense and a physical presence.

6. **Cookie** - The Reliable Goalie

Points (PTS): 23 | Games Played (GP): 1 | SV%: 0.885 | GAA: 3.84

Cookie is one of the best goaltenders available, with a solid save percentage of 0.885 and a GAA of 3.84. His potential as a netminder is undeniable. Teams in need of a reliable goalie will find Cookie a valuable addition to their roster.

7. **Pluds** - The Versatile Forward

Points (PTS): 53 | Games Played (GP): 8 | Goals (G): 42 | PPG: 4.08

Pluds has the skills to thrive both offensively and physically. With 42 goals in just 8 games, his scoring ability is clear. While he will not be playing goalie this season, Pluds’ well-rounded skill set and physicality (255 hits) make him a strong forward prospect.

8. **Zaz** - The Steady Presence

Points (PTS): 11 | Games Played (GP): 8 | SV%: 0.806

Zaz may not have the highest offensive numbers, but his strong performance as a goalie makes him a great pick for any team looking to shore up their defense. His 0.806 save percentage indicates he has the potential to be a reliable backstop in net.

9. **Shug** - The Shutdown Defender

Points (PTS): 9 | Games Played (GP): 3 | SV%: 0.855

Shug might be a quieter pick compared to others, but his 0.855 save percentage and strong defensive presence make him a solid choice for any team in need of a goalie with great reflexes and consistency.

10. **Winner** - The Underdog Player

Points (PTS): 5 | Games Played (GP): 0 | SV%: 0.836

Winner may have a lower points total, but with a respectable save percentage of 0.836, he’s a hidden gem for any team looking to take a chance on an up-and-coming goalie. Though his playing time has been limited, his potential as a netminder could shine through with more opportunities.

Conclusion

The draft is filled with potential stars, from offensive juggernauts like Tendy and Clifford to the elite goaltenders like Cookie and Shug. Teams will need to weigh their options carefully as they build their rosters, and the top 10 picks should all make a significant impact in the upcoming season. Who will be the next breakout star? Time will tell!
