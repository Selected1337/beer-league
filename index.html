Spotlight: Brick McMoss

MOSSWOOD — When the Mossy Villagers first signed Brick McMoss out of the Vine League five years ago, nobody expected much from the slow-talking, moss-covered goaltender from the Swampfront Hamlet. A quiet Villager with roots deeper than the jungle trees he trained under, McMoss was seen more as a mascot than a menace between the pipes.

Fast forward to Season 6, and the name Brick McMoss echoes through the Overworld like a creeper hissing in sudden death overtime.

At 6’3” and clad in custom-carved moss-dyed armor, McMoss has become the literal wall behind the Villagers’ rise to playoff contention. He leads the league in shutouts (7) and is second in both save percentage (.934) and goals against average (1.72). And yet, it’s not just the numbers that define him — it’s the moments.

Whether it was his triple-overtime stop against the Mavericks with nothing but a wooden stick and sheer instinct, or the diving pad save that robbed the Krakens’ Bloop McGill in front of a packed Netherdome, McMoss has made a habit of stealing wins from the jaws of the void.

"He's like trying to shoot through cobblestone," said Redstone Raptors captain Flint Sparkman. "No give. No cracks. Just... Moss."

McMoss attributes his success to his pre-game routine: drinking swamp stew, meditating by a glow lichen pond, and reinforcing his pads with reinforced mud blocks for “extra groundedness.”

Despite his newfound stardom, McMoss remains humble. When asked about his fame during a recent press conference, he simply said:

"Goal good. Puck bad. Me stop puck."

Truer words were never grunted.
